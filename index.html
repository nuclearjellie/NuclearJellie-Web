Taniti Cultural Festival:
Celebrate Polynesian heritage with traditional dance performances, craft workshops, and Polynesian music concerts held across Papeete and surrounding villages.

Full Moon Polynesian Dance Night:
Experience an enchanting evening of traditional dance and fire performances under the full moon at Matira Beach.

Quick Facts:

Taniti is a small tropical island in the Pacific.

The island is less than 500 square miles.

Taniti has both sandy and rocky beaches.

Varied terrain including tropical rainforests and a mountainous interior.

The island is home to an active volcano.

Has a indigenous population of about 20,000.

The Tanitian economy includes fishing, agriculture and tourism.

Bora Bora:
 Renowned for its luxury overwater bungalows, pristine beaches, and vibrant lagoon.

Moorea:
 Known for its lush landscapes, vibrant marine life, and proximity to Taniti.

Huahine:
 Often cited for its natural beauty and tranquil, untouched atmosphere.

Cascades de Faarumai:
 The three waterfalls are located in the village of Tiarei.

Arahoho Blowhole:
 Perched along the rocky coast of Taniti and bordered by a scenic black sand beach.

Museum of Taniti and The Islands:
 A modern museum that uses mixed media to tell the story of French Polynesia.

Moorea Street Food Tour:
 A great place to find authentic Tanitian food.
